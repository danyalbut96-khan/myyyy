AI Insights: Plain Language Summary

"Paracetamol is a widely used over-the-counter medicine primarily taken to lower high temperatures and relieve various types of pain, from headaches to joint stiffness. Unlike Ibuprofen, it doesn't reduce inflammation, but it's generally gentler on the stomach. It works by blocking chemical messengers in the brain that tell us we are in pain and helps regulate the body's internal thermostat."

Dosage & Usage

schedule

Standard Adult Dose

500mg - 1000mg every 4-6 hours.

priority_high

Maximum Dose

4000mg (4 grams) in 24 hours.

Contraindications

Severe Liver Disease: Significant risk of hepatotoxicity.

Alcohol Consumption: Heavy drinkers should consult a doctor due to liver stress.

Side Effects

Nausea Stomach Pain Loss of Appetite Rash (Rare)
